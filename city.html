Problem Statement

Jaipur faces recurring water scarcity issues due to its semi-arid climate. There is a need for an integrated plan focusing on rainwater harvesting, water recycling, and sustainable usage. Jaipur, the capital city of Rajasthan in India, faces significant water scarcity due to its semi-arid climate and limited natural freshwater resources.

Business Cases

Innovative water management solutions have the potential to address not only the water scarcity problem but also generate business opportunities through water-saving technologies and sustainable practices.

City Plan

Jaipur's city plan to address water scarcity involves a combination of modern infrastructure development, community participation, and technology-driven solutions. The city is divided into zones for targeted interventions, ensuring resources are allocated efficiently and effectively.

  • Installing smart water meters across households to monitor usage and minimize waste.
  • Developing centralized reservoirs to store and distribute harvested rainwater.
  • Creating green spaces and permeable surfaces to aid groundwater recharge.
  • Implementing zonal water management strategies to ensure equitable distribution.

SDG Rules

Complying with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) related to water conservation, sanitation, and efficient resource utilization to ensure a sustainable future for urban environments.
